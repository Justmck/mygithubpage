Hi, I'm Justin.

I'm a product designer specializing in UI/UX from the small town of Woodinville, WA. I currently attend the University of Washington as an early-admit to the Information School studying Human-Computer Interaction. While currently sharpening my design skills, my goal in the next few years is to become just as proficient at front-end development so that I can be involved in the product creation cycle from beginning to end. When not designing, you can find me trying to get the perfect shot with my trusty Nikon p900, cheering on the Seahawks every Sunday, or out tasting different coffee shops around Seattle.

Inspiration

Giving credit where credit is due; these are a few of the individuals I have looked up to, and whose works and writing have inspired me to chose design as a career. Spanning a wide variety of mediums both physical and digital, I consider these individuals (and their teams of course) to be great sources of advice and guidance to anyone who is just starting out as I have.

Chen Ye

Visual identity at Hack@Brown, accurate design industry foresight, and using good design to explain complex concepts; Chen is a crazy-skilled college designer.

Julie Zhuo

One of the best design writers on Medium; Julie has given some great insight into how to make the best of my time in school, as well as well as pearls of wisdom from her years in the field.

Stefan Hiienurm

Having one of my all-time favorite Portfolio designs, being one of my top Twitter design article posters, and breath-taking drone photography has helped Stefan top my list of inspirations.

Andrew Wilkinson

Co-founder of the design agency behind a certain team chat app among countless other works. Andrew is also the publisher of the Designer News online community.

AJ

AJ and his work at Pixelarity and HTML5UP has been crucial in sparking my interest in front-end development. Poking around the source files has provided me several examples of what makes a great website.

Tobias van Schneider

The man who collaborated with Collins to create what I consider to be the greatest branding redesign in the past decade. Oh, and his legendary facial hair is pretty inspirational too.

Sam Brown

I have immense respect for those who pull off a branding turnaround, and Sam did just that with Foursquare. Top-notch quality, and has turned me into a loyal fan of the service.

Jeff Hilnbrand

Founder and design lead at Bitcamp; starting one of the best-designed collegiate hackathons is impressive enough. But starting his own agency as a senior? Bravo.
